QGIS Map Projections

Until about a week ago, I didn't know there was something called a map projection or that it differed from a map. I took for granted that when maps represent the physical world at one scale or another, they rely on techniques which cause distortions. Recently, mapping techniques have improved a great deal and modern maps often hybridize several mapping styles to produce what we consider a normal looking map. Several of the first maps shown on this page look fairly normal. As you go further down, different mathematical orientations and priorities have different consequences for the appearance of the map. This is largely due to the earth being treated as a sphere, spheroid, and geoid respectively by different map projections. In QGIS, I implemented Indicatrix's various projections and learned about the effects of these different map-making approaches. I saw orientations which privileged one variable, like an axis, or several and thus formed hybrid maps. The last map I attempted to produce was a sort of regional map on the Pacific Ocean and the landmasses which border it. I learned that this would probably be better projected onto a more local map layer.

3857 Pseudo Mercator

This cylindrical map projection is done on a sphere using an ellipsoid's semi-major axis. It conserves proportions at the poles, unlike the world cylindrical map shown below.

53018 Winkel Tripel

Oscar Winkel began developing this projetion in the early 20th century. Winkel projections are modified from azumathal projections. There is an azumathal projection several maps below.

54034 World Cylindrical

This cylindrical map heavily distorts proportions at the poles.

54027 Conic

Conic map projections view the world from the perspective of a pole, most often the North pole.

102016 Azumathal

This conic map conserves the South Pole's continuity as a land mass unlike the first conic map.

53076 Wagner VII

This map is similar to the Pseudo Mercator in that it maintains proportions between land masses and oceans well, while representing the earth as a spheroid.
